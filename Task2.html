Konidala Pawan Kalyan[2] (born Konidala Kalyan Babu; 2 September[a]) is an Indian politician and actor serving as the 9th deputy chief minister of Andhra Pradesh since 12 June 2024.[3] He is also serving as the Minister of Panchayati Raj, Rural Development & Rural Water Supply, Environment, Forest, and Science & Technology in the Government of Andhra Pradesh.[4] He is the founder and president of the Jana Sena Party. As an actor Kalyan primarily works in Telugu cinema and is known for his unique acting style and mannerisms. He has a large fan base and is one of the highest-paid actors of Indian cinema. He has been featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list multiple times since 2013. Kalyan is the recipient of a Filmfare Award South and a SIIMA Award among other accolades.[8] Kalyan made his acting debut in the 1996 film Akkada Ammayi Ikkada Abbayi but rose to prominence with the dramas Gokulamlo Seeta (1997) and Suswagatham (1998). He achieved stardom for his performance in Tholi Prema (1998), which won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Telugu that year. Kalyan established himself as a leading actor with successful projects such as Thammudu (1999), Badri (2000), Kushi (2001), Balu (2005), Jalsa (2008), Gabbar Singh (2012), Attarintiki Daredi (2013), Gopala Gopala (2015), Vakeel Saab (2021), and Bheemla Nayak (2022).[9] He received the Filmfare Award for Best Actor â€“ Telugu for Gabbar Singh while Attarintiki Daredi held the record of being the highest-grossing Telugu film of all time.[10][11] In addition to acting, he produces films under the banners Anjana Productions and Pawan Kalyan Creative Works.[12] In 2008, Kalyan entered into politics as the youth wing president of his brother Chiranjeevi's Praja Rajyam Party, but he left after it merged into the Congress party. Education

Kalyan was born as Konidala Kalyan Babu[21] on 2 September[a] in a Telugu family to Konidala Venkata Rao and Anjana Devi in Bapatla, Andhra Pradesh.[26][27] Venkata Rao worked as a police constable and was transferred on a regular basis. Kalyan was educated at the St. Joseph's High School in Nellore.[28] H e was awarded the title "Pawan" during one of his public martial arts presentations.[29] He also holds a black belt in Karate. Pawan Kalyan, a prominent actor, politician, and social activist, has been a significant figure in Andhra Pradesh, particularly in the realm of education. Known for his charismatic presence and dedicated public service, Pawan Kalyan has consistently emphasized the importance of education in transforming society. As the leader of the Jana Sena Party, he has advocated for educational reforms and better infrastructure in the state's educational institutions. Born as Konidela Kalyan Babu on September 2, 1971, in Bapatla, Andhra Pradesh, Pawan Kalyan grew up understanding the challenges faced by the educational sector in rural and semi-urban areas. His early education was completed in Andhra Pradesh, and he later moved to Chennai for further studies. Despite his passion for the arts and his eventual career in the film industry, he never lost sight of the significance of education. Pawan Kalyan's entry into politics was driven by his desire to address the socio-economic issues in Andhra Pradesh, with education being one of his top priorities. He believes that a well-educated population is essential for the holistic development of the state. Under his leadership, the Jana Sena Party has put forward various initiatives aimed at improving the quality of education and making it accessible to all segments of society. One of Pawan Kalyan's key proposals has been the establishment of more government schools with better facilities, particularly in rural areas. He has stressed the need for qualified teachers, adequate infrastructure, and modern teaching aids to enhance the learning experience for students. His vision includes the integration of technology in classrooms to provide students with a contemporary education that prepares them for global challenges. In addition to advocating for primary and secondary education, Pawan Kalyan has also focused on higher education. He has called for the establishment of new universities and the improvement of existing ones to ensure that students in Andhra Pradesh have access to world-class higher education. Scholarships and financial aid for economically disadvantaged students are also a part of his educational reforms agenda. Pawan Kalyan's commitment to education is also reflected in his efforts to address the issues of student suicides and mental health. Recognizing the immense pressure faced by students, he has proposed counseling services and mental health programs in schools and colleges. He believes that a supportive and nurturing educational environment is crucial for the overall well-being and success of students. Furthermore, Pawan Kalyan has been vocal about the need for vocational training and skill development programs. He understands that not all students may pursue academic careers and that vocational training can provide them with the necessary skills to secure employment and contribute to the economy. His initiatives aim to bridge the gap between education and employability, ensuring that students are equipped with practical skills that are in demand in the job market. Pawan Kalyan's efforts in the educational sector have garnered widespread support and appreciation from various quarters. His holistic approach to education, encompassing primary, secondary, higher, and vocational education, highlights his understanding of the diverse needs of students. By focusing on educational reforms, Pawan Kalyan aims to create a knowledgeable and skilled workforce that can drive the state's progress and development. In conclusion, Pawan Kalyan's dedication to improving education in Andhra Pradesh is evident through his various initiatives and proposals. His vision for an educated and skilled society underscores the critical role of education in achieving socio-economic development. As he continues to champion the cause of education, Pawan Kalyan remains a beacon of hope for many in Andhra Pradesh who aspire for a better and brighter future through the power of learning.

Acting career

Karunakaran-directed Tholi Prema (1999) which won the National Award and six Nandi Awards that year.[31] After Tholi Prema, Kalyan acted in Thammudu, playing the role of a kick boxer. Political career

Jana Sena Party Kalyan founded a political party named Jana Sena Party on 14 March 2014.[66] He wrote a book titled Ism which is also the ideology of the Jana Sena party.[67] He met with then Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) Prime ministerial candidate Narendra Modi to discuss the issues related to both the Telugu states and extended his support.[68] He campaigned extensively for the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) and BJP alliance in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. He opposed Congress party's rule stating the slogan Congress Hatao, Desh Bachao (lit.â€‰'Stop Congress, Save the Country' in Hindi).[69] His rallies drew what the Deccan-Journal called "huge crowds" in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.[70] In August 2017, he announced that he intended to enter full-time politics starting October 2017 once he completes his film commitments.[71] Kalyan brought the Uddanam kidney disease crisis to the attention of media and politicians through protests and a hunger strike. Government of Andhra Pradesh responded by constructing dialysis centers and implementing various schemes for the village.[72][73] In November 2016, Kalyan announced that Jana Sena will contest in the 2019 general elections in Andhra Pradesh.[74] He stated that he was planning to contest from all 175 legislatures of Andhra Pradesh without any alliance.[75] He opposed central government's move to privatise Dredging Corporation of India (DCI)[76] on a golden platter.[77] Kalyan led a protest march to condolence farmers who committed suicides or migrated from the drought-prone regions of Rayalaseema.[78] He opposed the TDP Government's decision on land pooling.[79] Kalyan conducted a march on the historical Dowleswaram Barrage in Rajahmundry demanding political accountability.[80] He exposed the alleged unchecked mining in the reserve forest area at Vanthada village of Prathipadu in East Godavari district.[81] Kalyan has announced the Jana Sena party's 2019 election manifesto at Rajahmundry[82] public meeting with many measures to improve the lives of farmers,[83] farm labourers, women, youth, and students. His party would contest together with left parties[84] Communist Party of India, Communist Party of India (Marxist), and Bahujan Samaj Party[85] for the upcoming 2019 elections in both Telugu states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. At the same time, when he was actively campaigning for the elections all around Andhra Pradesh, he fell sick and shared a few health problems. As he was getting ready for his next round of campaign at Sattenapalle, he felt dizzy and nauseated. When the chopper was down for refilling at Gannavaram airport, he was down with vomiting, dehydration, and drowsiness. Immediately then, he was hospitalized and treated in Vijayawada. Again the campaign resumed after he got well.[86][87] The Jana Sena Party has contested 140 constituencies in the 2019 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly elections. Kalyan has contested two constituencies â€“ Gajuwaka[88] and Bhimavaram.[89] He has lost in both to candidates from YSR Congress Party.[90] His party was able to win from Razole, making it the only seat it has won in the election.[91] Later the same year, on 3 November 2019, Kalyan led a long march in Visakhapatnam in support of construction workers against YSR Congress Party governance who have been facing unemployment due to shortage of supply of sand in Andhra Pradesh.[92] On 16 January 2020, Kalyan has announced his party's alliance with BJP, after three years of distancing from it. Both the parties fought together in elections of 2024.[93] On 12 February 2020, he led a rally for justice to Sugali Preethi, a 15-year-old girl who was raped and murdered, in Kurnool.[94] He demanded an inquiry by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) into the incident.[95] The Jana Sena Party contested 21 constituencies in the 2024 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly election, with Kalyan contesting in Pithapuram constituency. He won by a margin of more than 70,000 votes; Jana Sena Party won in all the contested twenty-one assembly constituencies and two Lok Sabha seats.[96] On 12 June 2024 Kalyan was sworn in as a cabinet minister of Andhra Pradesh, whereas on 16 June 2024, he was announced as the deputy chief minister.[97]
